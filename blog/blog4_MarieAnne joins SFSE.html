Undergraduate BREE student joins SFSE Lab for summer internship

May 01, 2023

On May 01, 2023, the SFSE Research group welcomed a new undergraduate student- Miss Marie-Anne Dessureault to the team.

Miss Dessureault is currently an undergraduate student at the Department of Bioresource Engineering, McGill University-Macdonald Campus. Although she is in my first year of engineering training, research has been a component of my CEGEP and undergraduate studies.

At CEGEP, she conducted an in-depth literature review on various topics around economics, sociology, art interpretation in the 20th century, politics and history, resulting in high-quality reports.

For example, in her Psychology class in CEGEP, Miss Dessureault investigated the influence of the number of factors that influence a defined decision made by an individual. In this, she designed and performed surveys to confirm the hypothesis on CEGEP students, translating the results into technical reports.

At the undergraduate level, Miss Dessureault research experience stems from designing and analyzing a bioprocess for plant-based food stock in Bioresource Engineering Design 1 class. In this course, she worked with a team of four students to reuse food waste from grocery stores and transform them into other stock. To achieve this, her team employed SuperPro Designer V12 design, simulated and analyzed a bioprocess plant which utilized supermarket vegetable overflow and transformed them into vegetable broth powder.

.
She also investigated the potential economic and energy savings of the proposed design against the conventional treatment of leftover vegetables in supermarkets. The results of this research were translated into a technical report.

She will be working on the Environmental-Nutriomics research space with support from Mr. Prince Agyemang on DISH and on feed additives in the dairy industry along with other projects.

Miss Dessureault is passionate about sustainability, circular economy, and communication. In her leisure time, she prefers cooking and creating art.
